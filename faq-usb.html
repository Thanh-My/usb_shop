USB FAQs

A USB flash drive is a small, plug and play portable storage device which, unlike an optical drive or traditional hard drive, has no moving parts.

The USB is made up of 3 components.

  • Body/Model
  • Controller
  • Memory

The purpose of a flash drive is to store files. A USB flash drive can be used in place of a CD or DVD. But please keep in mind that USB flash drives are different from CDs and DVDs. Unlike CDs & DVDs, there is no standard for how files are stored on a USB flash drive. The way a machine accesses the files on a flash drive will not be consistent.

The controller is a physical unit that acts as the brain on the drive. It knows how much space is available and will connect with the machine. Different drives have different controllers.

Our standard is 2.0, but if you need something different (like 3.0, for example) give us a call! One of our product specialists can set up a custom quote for you.

*Please be aware that large HD files with high resolution may have issues transferring through an older 1.0 or 2.0 USB connection.

We currently offer 1gb, 2gb, 4gb, 8gb, 16gb, 32GB, 64GB, 128GB & 256GB.

Drive sizes of up to 16GB can be self-quoted on our website. For larger sizes please inquire with your Product Specialist.

The standard formatting is FAT32.

If any individual file is 4gb or greater, the drive will be formatted as EX-FAT.

We can format the drive as NTFS, however, this is not a standard and would need to be requested. Please note that a drive formatted as NTFS will not work on a MAC since NTFS is a Microsoft format.
